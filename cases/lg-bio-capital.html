Client website

LG Bio Capital Partners

A website for a life science advisory company where the main goal was to make a complex professional field feel calm, credible and easy to understand.

View live site Contact me

Problem

The company needed a professional online presence that could explain its advisory work without making the content feel heavy or difficult to scan.

Target group

Life science companies, investors and professional partners who need to understand the company quickly and trust the information.

My role

Web design, frontend and content structure. I focused on layout, hierarchy, navigation and making the content easier to follow.

Tools

HTML, CSS, responsive layout work, content hierarchy and visual design decisions.

Process

Making complex content feel calmer

1. Structure first

I started by looking at what a visitor needs first: what the company does, who it helps and why it is credible. That helped shape a clearer page order.

2. Visual direction

I kept the design calm and professional with large whitespace, a restrained color direction and a hero section that feels more advisory than promotional.

3. Frontend implementation

The layout was built to work across screen sizes, with a strong hero, readable content sections and navigation that stays simple.

Decisions

Design and code choices

Design choice

I used a calm visual hierarchy because the subject is already complex. The design should support trust and clarity, not compete with the content.

Code choice

I kept the frontend structure straightforward, so the site is easier to maintain and explain. The focus was on responsive layout and readable sections.

Testing and iteration

I checked the site visually across screen sizes and adjusted spacing, image handling and hierarchy. A formal usability test would be a useful next step.

Result

What the project shows

The final website gives the company a clearer and more professional first impression. In my portfolio, this project shows that I can work with real client material, structure content and build a calm frontend presentation.
